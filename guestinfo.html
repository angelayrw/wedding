Queens' College

The college has two entrances, with the main entrance located on Silver Street. Upon entering, please speak to the porter, who will provide directions. You’ll also find signs to guide you along the way.

Accommondations

There are numerous hotels within walking distance of Queens’ College. The nearest ones include the Graduate Hotel , Hilton , and Hotel du Vin . For serviced apartments, Turing Locke is a recommended option. Queens’ College also offers a limited number of single/twin ensuite rooms; please contact Angela if interested.

Travel

The most convenient way to travel from London to Cambridge is by train. Trains run regularly from King’s Cross, St Pancras, and Liverpool Street stations. We recommend booking your tickets via Trainline. Within Cambridge, bus services are limited. Uber is an efficient option, or you might prefer Panther Taxi , a local provider.

Things to Do

Cambridge boasts 31 unique colleges. Apart from Queens’, we recommend King’s College, Trinity College, and St John’s College as the most popular ones. You may need to purchase tickets onsite at each college. Punting is a popular Cambridge activity. There are various providers, including one right in front of Queens’ College. For those interested in city history, we recommend a guided city walk.

Restaurants

As steak lovers, we suggest Maison du Steak. For a great view and brunch menu, try Six at the Varsity Hotel. If you’re in the mood for Chinese cuisine, Angela recommends Seven Days on Regent Street.
